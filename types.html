    Types of Primary Sources

    There is a wide range of different types of primary sources. Primary sources are not just limited to printed text. Print is just one of the many different formats possible.

    Important Note on Newspaper Articles

    News and newspaper articles are a unique type of source - sometimes they are primary sources and sometimes secondary sources. There are some important things to consider when deciding whether a newspaper article is a primary source or not:

    When was it written?

    News articles written during the events they describe are more likely to be considered primary sources versus news articles that recount events from the past - separated by a long interval of time.

    What type of article is it?

    Editorial or Opinion articles offer commentary and perspective on the news, but are not considered to be first-hand accounts of events.

    Examples of Different Primary Sources

    Original Document
    Déclaration des Droits de l'Homme et du Citoyen de 1789

    This purpose of this document was to create the Treasury Department with the purpose of managing government revenue for the United States.

    Date

    1789

    Source

    Bibliothèque nationale de France

    What makes this a primary source?

    Content (text) was created during the event it describes. The text of the Declaration of the rights of man and citizen was drafted in 1793, during the events of the French Revolution. The important aspect of this primary source is the text, which can appear in different formats, such as a webpage, but still qualifies as a primary source.

    Letter (Correspondence)
    Letter from Winston Churchill to Neville Chamberlain

    Letter from Winston Churchill in the role of First Lord of the Admiralty to Neville Chamberlain, describing the effort to mobilize for war against Germany.

    Date

    October 1, 1939

    Source

    Churchill Papers, Churchill Archives Centre, Cambridge, U.K.

    What makes this a primary source?

    It is a personal correspondence from Winston Churchill during World War II offering a direct account of events taking place at the time.

    Photograph
    Cross border commuters in the Berlin area

    Photograph of a border commuters at Berlin S-Bahnhof Düppel, on the southern border of West Berlin and the Brandenburg district of the GDR (1955).

    Date

    September 1, 1955

    Source

    Deutsches Bundesarchiv

    What makes this a primary source?

    The photo was taken in a specific point of time in history that depicts an historical event or information.

    Newspaper Article
    The Budapest uprising events recounted hour by hour by a group of French journalists

    French newspaper (Figaro) account of events during the Budapest uprising in 1956 with an hour-by-hour account of events.

    Date

    November 13, 1956

    Source

    Figaro/RFE/RL Collection - Media Coverage

    What makes this a primary source?

    The newspaper article provides eye-witness accounts of the events happening during the uprising in November 1956 by news correspondents who are there as the events are happening.

    Statistical Surveys and Data
    Census Records from the British National Archives

    A page from the 1851 census. This page, from the enumeration books, shows part of the head count at Buckingham Palace

    Date

    1851

    Source

    The National Archives

    What makes this a primary source?

    Information was collected during a census in 1851 that recorded data about people living in England and information about them such as size of household, age, and sex.

    Statistical Surveys and Data
    Map of Berlin with Allied and Soviet Zones

    A map produced during the blockade of Berlin in 1948-49 by the British Government to depict the Zones of control for the Allied powers and Soviet Union.

    Date

    1949

    Source

    Foreign & Commonwealth Office, held under Trust Deed by British Library

    What makes this a primary source?

    The map was produced during the events of the Berlin blockade and containing historical information.

    Political Cartoon
    Political Cartoon entitled "Deutschlands Zukunft" (Germany's Future)

    This political cartoon was created during the time of Germany unification. It asks how will all the german regions fit under one hat, a reference to the challenge of uniting the country and the power of Prussia at the time.

    Date

    August 22, 1870

    Source

    Kikeriki

    What makes this a primary source?

    The political cartoon was created during the events about which it is commenting on. It offers insight into the historical situation at the time through the cartoon and the comments being made using satire.

    Audio Visual
    Documentary on the Solidarity Movement in Poland during the 1980s

    This documentary contains video footage of the events the happened in the early 1980s in Poland including the strike of the Gdansk shipyard workers.

    Date

    1981

    Source

    solidarnosc.org.pl

    What makes this a primary source?

    The audio visual footage was created during the historical events described in the documentary. The video constitutes an eyewitness perspective on the events.
